I am a principal researcher at [Microsoft Research New England](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/lab/microsoft-research-new-england/) where I work on the [BioML team](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/theme/biomedical-ml/). My research focuses on developing machine learning methods for high-impact scientific and healthcare applications, with current emphasis on three areas:

**Computational Pathology & Foundation Models.** I lead research on large-scale foundation models for digital pathology, including the [Virchow](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-024-03141-0) and [Virchow2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.00738) tile-level models and the [Prism](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.10254) and [Prism2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13063) multi-modal slide-level models. This work was published in *Nature Medicine* and enables clinical-grade computational pathology and rare cancer detection.

**Disease Progression Modeling.** I develop probabilistic models that discover disease subtypes and predict progression from longitudinal clinical data. This includes work on [Parkinson's disease](https://www.thelancet.com/journals/landig/article/PIIS2589-7500(21)00101-1/fulltext) (published in *The Lancet Digital Health*) and [ALS](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43588-022-00299-w) (published in *Nature Computational Science*).

**Machine Learning for Science.** I apply ML to accelerate scientific discovery, including [data-driven prediction of battery cycle life](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41560-019-0356-8) (published in *Nature Energy*, 3000+ citations) and [Bayesian optimization for sustainable materials design](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0008884623002995).

Previously, I was a postdoc at the [Center for Computational Health](https://researcher.watson.ibm.com/researcher/view_group.php?id=6743) and a research staff member in the [MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab](https://mitibmwatsonailab.mit.edu) at IBM Research. I received my PhD in Chemical Engineering from MIT, where I worked in the [Braatz lab](http://web.mit.edu/braatzgroup/), and my BS from Carnegie Mellon University.
